Labour Law

Labour law oversees the employer-employee relationship, including individual employment contracts, the application of wrongdoing and contract doctrines, and a large group of constitutional regulation.

Labour law oversees the employer-employee relationship, including individual employment contracts, the application of wrongdoing and contract doctrines, and a large group of constitutional regulation on issues such as the right to organize and negotiate collective bargaining agreements, protection from discrimination, wages and hours, and health and safety.

Maritime law, also known as admiralty law, the branch of international law that deals with territorial and international waters or with shipping or offenses occurring on open water. International rules, governing the use of the oceans and seas, are known as the Law of the Sea.

Real Estate

Real estate law is the area of law that governs buying, using and selling land. It is the law that governs how people acquire property and what they can do with the property that they own. Real estate law is also called real property law.

Corporate

Learn more

Corporate law is the body of laws, rules, regulations and practices that govern the formation and operation of corporations. It is the body of law that regulates legal entities that exist to conduct business.
